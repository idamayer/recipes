# Carrot Curry Lentil Soup

> A velvety, sun-hued purée where the earthy sweetness of carrots and red lentils melts into a richly spiced, coconut-infused broth.

## Ingredients

- 3 Tbs vegetable oil, divided
- 2 cups chopped onions *(about 2 small onions)*
- 1 cup dried red lentils
- 3 medium carrots, peeled and roughly chopped *(about 1 cup)*
- 14-oz. can coconut milk
- 1 bay leaf
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1-inch piece fresh ginger, minced
- 1 Tbs curry powder, preferably hot
- 1 tsp salt *(plus more to taste)*
- 2 tsp fresh cilantro, chopped *(optional)*

---

## To Serve

- Fresh cilantro leaves
- Warm pita bread, naan, or crusty artisan bread
- A scoop of cooked brown rice *(optional)*
- Lime wedges

---

## Method

### 01 — Build the Base & Simmer

In a medium saucepan or heavy-bottomed pot, heat 2 tablespoons of the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook, stirring frequently, until they soften and begin to turn a golden brown—about 10 minutes. Pour in 4 cups of water, then stir in the red lentils, chopped carrots, coconut milk, 1 teaspoon of salt, and the bay leaf. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover partially and let it simmer gently until the lentils and carrots are completely tender, about 20 minutes.

### 02 — Bloom the Aromatics

While the soup base is simmering, prepare your flavor accent. In a small separate skillet over medium heat, warm the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil. Add the minced garlic, minced ginger, curry powder, and optional cilantro. Cook, stirring constantly, for about 2 minutes. Watch closely and pull the pan off the heat the moment the spices bloom into an intensely fragrant, golden paste—don't let the garlic scorch. Immediately stir this aromatic mixture directly into the simmering soup pot.

### 03 — Puree until Velvety

Remove and discard the bay leaf. Transfer the soup in careful batches to a food processor or standing blender (or use an immersion blender directly in the pot). Blend until the texture is completely uniform and velvety smooth. Taste the soup, adding a pinch more salt if needed to make the curry notes pop. Return to the heat briefly if needed, and serve steaming hot.

---

## A Note from the Kitchen

*This soup gets incredibly thick and luxurious once pureed. If you prefer a thinner consistency, feel free to whisk in a splash of warm vegetable stock or water at the end. It's a wonderful make-ahead meal because the flavors deepen beautifully overnight in the fridge—just reheat gently and finish with a fresh squeeze of lime to wake it back up.*
