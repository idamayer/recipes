## Description

These potatoes are famous for their extraordinary contrast of texture: a deeply crisp crust surrounding a fluffy interior. The technique relies on parboiling with baking soda, roughing up the surface, and roasting in hot fat until golden.

**Adapted from** [*Kenji López-Alt, Serious Eats*](https://www.seriouseats.com/the-best-roast-potatoes-ever-recipe)

---

## Ingredients

- 4 lb Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into large chunks
- Kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 5 tbsp olive oil, duck fat, goose fat, or beef fat
- 1 small handful fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- Freshly ground black pepper

---

## To Serve

- Extra flaky salt

---

## Method

### 01 — Parboil the Potatoes

Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Season generously with salt and add the baking soda. Add the potatoes and cook until a knife meets only slight resistance, about 10 minutes after the water returns to a boil.

### 02 — Infuse the Fat

Meanwhile, combine the oil or fat with the rosemary and garlic in a small saucepan. Cook gently until the garlic just begins to turn golden. Strain, reserving both the infused fat and the garlic-rosemary mixture separately.

### 03 — Rough the Surface

Drain the potatoes thoroughly. Transfer to the pot, cover, and shake vigorously to rough up the exterior. The potatoes should develop a starchy paste-like coating.

### 04 — Roast

Toss the potatoes with the infused fat and spread on a rimmed baking sheet. Roast at 450°F (230°C), turning occasionally, until deeply golden and crisp on all sides, about 45–60 minutes.

### 05 — Finish

Toss the hot potatoes with the reserved garlic-rosemary mixture and black pepper. Season to taste and serve immediately.

---

## A Note from the Kitchen

*Duck fat produces the richest flavor, but olive oil works beautifully. The rougher the potato surface before roasting, the crispier the final result.*
