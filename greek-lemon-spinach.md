# Sour Greek Spinach with Chickpeas

> A vibrant, tangy medley where earthy spinach, sweet green onions, and spiced, pan-fried chickpeas are brought to life by the intense, bright punch of minced preserved lemon.

**Adapted from** *Med Deli (Chapel Hill, North Carolina)*

## Ingredients

- 20 oz frozen spinach *(2 packages)*
- 6+ stalks green onion, sliced into thin rounds
- 1 can chickpeas, rinsed and drained
- 2–3 tsp Ras El Hanout
- 2 preserved lemons, seeds removed
- ¼–½ tsp spray oil *(1–2 second spray)*
- Small sprinkling of sumac *(optional)*

> **On nutritional composition:** This nourishing dish yields approximately 510–550 calories, 32g of fat, and 35g of protein in total.

## Method

### 01 — Prep the Greens & Preserved Lemon

Place the frozen spinach and the sliced green onions into a microwave-safe bowl. Microwave the mixture just until the spinach is completely thawed and slightly warm to the touch. While the greens are warming, prepare the preserved lemon. Carefully remove and discard any seeds from the two lemons, then chiffonade and mince the rinds and flesh until they break down into a fine, paste-like mush.

### 02 — Crisp and Spice the Chickpeas

Lightly coat a skillet with a brief 1- to 2-second spray of oil (about ¼ to ½ teaspoon) and place it over medium heat. Add the rinsed and drained chickpeas along with a generous dusting of Ras El Hanout. Cook, stirring frequently, until the chickpeas are nicely coated, thoroughly warmed through, and fragrant from the bloomed spices. 

### 03 — Combine and Toss

Add the thawed spinach, green onions, and the minced preserved lemon mush directly into the skillet with the spiced chickpeas. Toss everything together vigorously until the ingredients are completely incorporated and the spinach is heated all the way through. Finish with an optional, light dusting of sumac for an extra layer of bright, fruity acidity, and serve immediately.

---

## A Note from the Kitchen

*This recipe is a home recreation of a beloved dish from Med Deli in Chapel Hill, North Carolina, inspired by fond memories of visiting with family. Preserved lemons provide a unique, salty-sour depth that regular lemon juice can't quite replicate, making it the absolute star of the dish. Using kitchen shears is a quick, mess-free way to slice through a large bundle of green onions directly over the bowl.*
