## Ingredients

- ½ cup salted brown butter room temperature
- ½ cup granulated sugar (scant)
- ½ cup brown sugar (not packed!)
- 2 large eggs room temperature
- 1 ½ teaspoon vanilla extract (measure with your heart)
- 1 ½ teaspoon cinnamon
- 1 pinch nutmeg
- 1 ½ cups all purpose flour
- 3/4 cup pecan meal plus more for dusting
- 1 ½ teaspoon baking powder
- ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
- ½ cup toasted pecans chopped

-----

## Method

### 01 — Line a baking sheet

Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

### 02 — Brown the butter

Brown the butter. Cool until firm (can be done by putting a larger pan/bowl of ice water under the container with the browned butter)

### 03 — Beat butter and sugars

In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment (or a large bowl with a hand mixer), beat together the brown butter, sugar, and brown sugar until fluffy and white. It should turn a noticeably different color. The double boiler of ice can also help achieve this.

### 04 — Add eggs and vanilla

Add in the eggs and vanilla and beat until just combined.

### 05 — Add dry ingredients

Add in the flour, pecan meal, baking powder, and salt and mix until just fully incorporated. Then stir in the toasted pecans. The dough will be sticky.

### 06 — Shape the dough

Scoop half the dough onto one side of the baking sheet and pat it into a 10 inch x 2 inch rectangle that’s about 1 inch thick - you will need to wet your hands a lot or it will be a huge mess and more Jackson Pollock than log. Repeat with the other half of the dough.

### 07 — Coat and chill

Coat the top of the loaf with pecan flour (as much as you can stick on, around 2 Tbsp to 1/4 cup per loaf)

Place the baking sheet with the shaped biscotti logs in the fridge and chill for about 20-30 minutes, or one TV episode (this helps them not spread too much).

### 08 — First bake

While the biscotti logs are chilling, preheat the oven to 350°F.

Bake at 350°F for 20 to 30 minutes, until the top and edges are set. Remove from the oven and let cool for 25 to 30 minutes.

### 09 — Slice

Carefully transfer the logs to a cutting board and use a spray bottle filled with room-temperature water to very lightly spritz them (just one spray on each section of the logs). Wait about 5 minutes before using a very sharp serrated knife to slice biscotti ½ inch wide.

### 10 — Second bake

Set the oven temperature to 315°F.

Return the slices to the baking sheet, standing them up with about ½ inch in between them, so air can circulate around them. Bake for another 20 to 30 minutes, until dry and golden.

### 11 — Cool

Remove from the oven and transfer to a wire rack to cool. Serve with coffee and enjoy!!

## A Note from the Kitchen

*Store biscotti in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 weeks.*

*To freeze biscotti: I recommend freezing the biscotti after the first bake. After the biscotti are sliced, freeze in an airtight container for up to 3 months. To finish baking, let thaw and then bake for 25 minutes as normal.*

*To make brown butter, melt unsalted butter over medium heat, stirring frequently. Watch closely for the butter to develop brown specs and a nutty aroma. Once you start seeing brown specs, let cook for just a few seconds longer (watch very carefully or it will burn). Remove from the heat and pour into a small bowl so that it doesn't continue to cook. Set aside and let cool for about 20 minutes in the fridge until soft and pliable. Note: For every ½ cup (1 stick or 113g), you'll lose about 1 tablespoon (14g) to evaporation while cooking. So you can either add in an extra 1 tablespoon before browning, or if you're browning big batches, just make up the weight difference in your recipe with regular unsalted butter if needed.*
