# Bulgogi Tacos

> Sweet, savory, and slightly spicy filling wrapped in a warm tortilla and finished with bright, creamy toppings.

## Description

This flexible weeknight meal borrows the sweet-savory profile of Korean bulgogi and pairs it with taco-style assembly. The filling is forgiving, scales well, and works equally well in tortillas or stuffed peppers.

---

## Ingredients

### Filling
- 1 lb ground meat
- ⅓–½ head cabbage, shredded or finely minced
- 2–3 carrots, shredded
- 1 onion, shredded *(optional)*
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated *(optional)*
- 1 lb tofu, crumbled *(optional)*
- Sesame seeds
- ⅓–½ cup applesauce
- 3–4 tbsp gochujang
- 4 tbsp soy sauce, plus more for deglazing
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- Optional: this can also be a good way to use up other sauces that are both savory and sweet like dumpling sauce, bbq, etc. The gochujang sauce is very forgiving.
- 2 tbsp neutral oil for the pan

### For Serving
- Tortillas or seeded, halved jalapeños
- Greek yogurt
- Cilantro
- Lemon juice or pickled radish
- Nutritional yeast

---

## Method

### 01 — Make the Sauce

Mix the applesauce, gochujang, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Warm briefly if needed to help the gochujang soften.

### 02 — Prepare the Filling

Shred the cabbage, carrots, and onion if using and add to a huge bowl. Mix the vegetables with the sauce. If using tofu, crumble it by hand into ground-meat-sized pieces and combine with the vegetables. After fully coating the vegetables in the sauce, mix in the meat, taking care to evenly incorporate it. Add in a generous amount of sesame seeds.

### 03 — Cook

Heat the neutral oil in a large skillet. Add the filling mixture and cook until browned and relatively dry. Add sesame oil if the mixture begins sticking, or near the end of cooking if it does not.

### 04 — Deglaze

When nearly finished, add additional soy sauce to the hot pan and scrape up any browned bits. Stir everything together thoroughly.

### 05 — Assemble

Warm tortillas in a skillet, or halve and seed peppers. Fill with the meat mixture. Top with lemon juice, cilantro, greek yogurt, and nutritional yeast - in that order for best structural stability

If using peppers, broil briefly and optionally top with cheese.
