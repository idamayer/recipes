# Italian Cream Cake

> A magnificent celebration centerpiece featuring layers of tender, coconut-flecked sponge studded with rich pecans and enveloped in a velvety, tangy cream cheese frosting.

## Ingredients

### For the Cake
- 5 eggs, separated and at room temperature
- 1 stick butter, room temperature
- ¾ cup sugar *(either ½ cup brown and ¼ cup white, or all white)*
- 2 cups white cake flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- ½ tsp salt
- 1 ¼ cups buttermilk
- 2 cups shredded coconut
- 2 cups finely chopped pecans
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

### For the Frosting
- 8 oz cream cheese *(1 package)*, room temperature
- ¼ cup milk or buttermilk
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- ¼ cup powdered sugar
- 1 Tbs cornstarch
- ¾ cup finely chopped pecans

---

## To Serve

- Extra toasted pecan halves for decoration
- A sprinkle of toasted shredded coconut
- Hot coffee or tea

## Method

### 01 — Prep and Whip the Whites

Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C) and grease two 9-inch round cake pans. In a perfectly clean bowl, whip the room-temperature egg whites until stiff peaks form, then carefully set them aside. *(Note: If your electric mixer has two bowls, you can comfortably save this step to execute alongside step 04 instead).*

### 02 — Mix the Batter Base

In the main bowl of your electric mixer, cream the room-temperature butter and sugar together until the mixture is light, fluffy, and pale. Add the 5 egg yolks and mix until the batter becomes completely smooth. From there, pour in the cake flour, baking soda, salt, and buttermilk, mixing just until combined.

### 03 — Master the Fold

Gently fold the whipped egg whites into the dense cake batter. Add the shredded coconut, finely chopped pecans, and vanilla extract, and fold once more. Your goal here is to achieve full incorporation without popping any of the delicate air bubbles in the whipped whites. 

> **On folding technique:** Slice your silicone spatula straight down into the center of the bowl, scoop up a glob of batter from the bottom, and gently rotate the spatula to lay it across the surface next to it—almost like turning the page of a book. What was on the bottom of the bowl is now on top. This may feel like an ineffectual way to mix at first, but keep laying down the unmixed portions and the batter will come together surprisingly fast.

### 04 — Bake and Cool

Divide the batter evenly between your two prepared cake pans. Bake for about 25 minutes, or until the tops are golden brown and a cake tester inserted into the dead center comes out clean. Let the cakes rest and cool inside their pans for about 10 minutes before turning them out onto a wire rack to finish cooling completely.

### 05 — Whip the Frosting

While the cake layers bake, construct the frosting. Whip the cream cheese, vanilla extract, and milk together until absolutely smooth and free of lumps, ideally using a stand mixer's whisk attachment. To avoid a messy cloud of sugar when adding the dry ingredients, turn off the mixer, add the powdered sugar and cornstarch, and manually fold a lump or two of the cream cheese over the top before turning the mixer back on. Fold in the pecans and store the frosting in the refrigerator until ready to assemble; it will firm up beautifully as it chills.

### 06 — Assemble and Finish

Once the cake layers are completely cold to the touch, place your first layer down on a serving plate. Spread a generous layer of the chilled cream cheese frosting across the top, stack the second layer over it, and use the remaining frosting to smoothly coat the top and sides of the completed cake.

---

## A Note from the Kitchen

*Using a blend of brown and white sugar in the cake batter lends a lovely, subtle caramel undertone that pairs beautifully with the earthy pecans and tropical coconut. Be absolutely sure your cake layers are completely cool before frosting them, otherwise the cream cheese frosting will soften and slip right off the sides.*
