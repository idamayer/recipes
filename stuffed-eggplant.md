# Chinese Stuffed Eggplant

> An underappreciated dim sum classic. Silky steamed eggplant filled with ground meat and topped with a savory oyster sauce sauce.

**Adapted from** *[The Woks of Life](https://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-stuffed-eggplant/)*

## Ingredients

### Eggplant
- 4 Japanese eggplants
- 9 oz ground pork
- 1 shredded zucchini
- ½ tsp soy sauce
- ⅛ tsp white pepper
- 1 tsp Shaoxing wine
- optional: 1 egg, if the mixture isn't sticking together enough
- optional: 2 scallions, finely chopped
- oil for shallow frying

### Sauce
- 2 Tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- 2 tsp water or chicken stock
(I usually eyeball the sauce)

---

## Method

### 01 — Make the Filling

Process the pork, seasonings, oils, and wine into a rough paste. Mix in the egg, and half the scallions. Refrigerate for 30 minutes.

### 02 — Stuff the Eggplant

Cut the eggplants into thick diagonal slices, making sure to match pairs of similar sizes. You may have a bit left of each end which you can mince and put in the meat mixture.  

### 03 — Stuff

Fill the eggplant slices with the meat mixture, creating little sandwiches.

### 04 — Brown

Pan-fry the stuffed eggplant until browned on both sides. Take care when flipping them so they don't fall open.

### 05 — Steam

Pour a few tablespoons of water into the pan and cover for 7-10 minutes, until tender.

### 06 —  Deglaze

Check the eggplants, if they are sticking, throw a splash of soy sauce into the pan to finish.

### 06 — Serve

Plate the eggplants. Mix up the sauce. Dollop sauce and remaining scallions on top.

---

## A Note from the Kitchen

*Ground chicken, turkey, or shrimp can be substituted for the pork.*
