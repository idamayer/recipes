# Pomegranate Molasses Chicken with Roasted Vegetables and Tzatziki

> [Persian-inspired chicken](https://immigrantstable.com/pomegranate-chicken/#recipe) with roasted veggies and optional yogurt sauce. Invented to find a use for the oft-abandoned grenadine syrup in the back of your cocktail shelf - it is a pomegranate syrup after all. This is an ideal recipe to meal prep the night before and put straight from the fridge into the oven when you get off work.

## Method

### 01 — Soak and Prepare

Soak the chickpeas overnight or ~6 hours (see directions below if you don't have time to soak).

### 02 — Marinate the Chicken

Preheat the oven to .
Place the chicken thighs one layer deep in a glass or ceramic baking pan. Combine grenadine, molasses, Tajín, cumin, turmeric, black pepper, clove, and balsamic vinegar into a marinade. Allow to marinate in the refrigerator ideally 1 hour, but could be 0 or overnight as your schedule dictates.

### 03 — Chop the vegetables

Cut vegetables into roughly 1 inch cubes. Toss with olive oil, salt, and red pepper flakes. Lay out on a sheet tray.

### 04 — Roast the Vegetables and Chicken

Roast the chicken and vegetables at 375°F for 40–45 minutes, flipping the vegetables once, until browned and tender. Check the chicken and take out when it reaches 165°F.

### 02 — Make the Tzatziki

If serving with Tzatziki: Grate the cucumber and squeeze out excess liquid. Zest and juice the lemon. Mix with yogurt, dill, salt, and pepper. Refrigerate until serving.

### 06 — Assemble

Layer rice, roasted vegetables, and chicken in bowls. Add tzatziki and finish with the roasted chickpeas.
