# Whey-Buckwheat Crepes with Salmon, Spinach, and Pepper Jack

> Tender crepes enriched with whey, folded around salmon, greens, and melting cheese for a meal that feels both practical and indulgent.

## Ingredients

### Crepes

- 1 to 1½ cups white flour
- 2–4 tablespoons buckwheat flour
- 1 egg
- About 2 cups whey, or water
- Pinch salt
- Butter or oil for the pan

### Filling

- 2 small salmon fillets
- Frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
- Pepper Jack cheese, sliced or shredded

---

## To Serve

- Green salad
- Fresh herbs
- Lemon wedges

## Method

### 01 — Make the Crepe Batter

Whisk together the white flour, buckwheat flour, egg, salt, and whey until the batter is very thin and pourable. Add more whey or water as needed until it reaches the consistency of light cream.

Let the batter rest for at least 15 minutes if time permits.

### 02 — Cook the Crepes

Heat a lightly buttered nonstick skillet over medium heat. Pour in a small amount of batter and immediately swirl to coat the pan in a thin layer.

Cook until the surface looks dry and the edges begin to lift, then flip and cook briefly on the second side. Repeat with the remaining batter.

### 03 — Prepare the Filling

Steam the salmon fillets until just cooked through and flaking easily.

Thaw the spinach completely and squeeze out as much water as possible.

Break the salmon into bite-sized pieces.

### 04 — Assemble

Lay a crepe flat and arrange pepper Jack cheese, salmon, and spinach over one half.

Fold in half, then fold again into quarters.

### 05 — Melt and Finish

Return the folded crepes to the skillet over medium-low heat and cook until the cheese melts and the filling is warmed through.

Serve immediately.

## A Note from the Kitchen

*Whey contributes a subtle tang and extra protein while putting a byproduct of cheesemaking or yogurt straining to good use. Water works perfectly well if whey is unavailable.*
