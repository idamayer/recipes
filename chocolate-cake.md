# Beatty's Chocolate Cake

> A deeply chocolatey layer cake with an exceptionally tender crumb and rich frosting, adapted for pantry-friendly instant coffee.

## Ingredients

- 1¾ cups all-purpose flour
- 1¾ cups sugar
- ¾ cup good cocoa powder
- 2 teaspoons baking soda
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 cup buttermilk, shaken
- ½ cup vegetable oil
- 2 extra-large eggs, at room temperature
- 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
- 1 cup hot water
- 2 teaspoons instant coffee powder

### Frosting

- Chocolate Buttercream Frosting (your preferred recipe)

---

## Method

### 01 — Prepare the Pans

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Butter two 8-inch round cake pans. Line with parchment paper, then butter and flour the pans.

### 02 — Mix the Dry Ingredients

Sift the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt into the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment. Mix on low speed until evenly combined.

### 03 — Add the Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, oil, eggs, and vanilla.

With the mixer running on low speed, slowly add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients.

Dissolve the instant coffee powder in the hot water. With the mixer still on low, slowly add the coffee mixture and mix just until combined, scraping the bottom of the bowl with a rubber spatula.

### 04 — Bake

Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans.

Bake for 35–40 minutes, until a cake tester inserted in the center comes out clean.

Cool in the pans for 30 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack and cool completely.

### 05 — Frost and Serve

Place one cake layer, flat side up, on a serving plate or cake stand. Spread a thin layer of frosting over the top.

Place the second layer on top, flat side up. Frost the sides and top evenly.

Cut into wedges and serve at room temperature.

---

## A Note from the Kitchen

*The original recipe uses 2 cups of sugar and freshly brewed coffee. This version reduces the sugar slightly to let the chocolate flavor come forward while maintaining the cake's moist texture. Instant coffee powder dissolved in hot water provides the same depth and bitterness that intensifies the cocoa without requiring brewed coffee.*

### Cupcake Variation

Bake in lined muffin tins for 25–30 minutes.
