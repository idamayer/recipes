# Suan Cai Yu

> Traditional [Sichuan soup](https://redhousespice.com/suan-cai-yu/#wprm-recipe-container-32512) of poached fish with uniquely delicious pickled mustard greens. This simplified version is one of our easy weeknight meals.

## Ingredients

- 1 lb frozen tilapia filets or other white fish
- 1 package pickled mustard greens
- 1" piece ginger
- 3 cups stock
- 2-3 Tbsp lard
- optional : 2 Tbsp sichuan peppercorns
- optional: 2 Tbsp Sichuan pickled chilies or other dried red pepper
- optional: 2 green onions
- optional: pinch of white pepper
- Optionally, serve with rice

---

## Method

### 01 — Prepare the Vegetables

Drain the brine off of the pickled vegetables and save it, set aside. Chop the pickled mustard greens. Then minge the ginger and green onions, if using.

### 02 — Fry the vegetables

Heat the lard and add in aromatics (ginger, sichuan peppercorn, chilies, green onion) to toast for 1 minute, then add in the pickled mustard greens. Fry until fragrant.

### 03 — Build the Broth

Add stock, reserved brine, and white pepper, and bring to a simmer.

### 04 — Cook the Fish

Add the fish and cook just until opaque and tender, about 1–2 minutes, or ~5 if using frozen.

### 05 — Flake the fish

You can tell the fish is done cooking because it will flake apart when pressed. As soon as it is cooked, use a knife or ladle to split each filet into bite size pieces. Serve with rice.

---

## A Note from the Kitchen

*The fish cooks very quickly. Overcooking causes it to become tough.*
